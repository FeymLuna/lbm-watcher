Parameters

Target aggressiveness55%
<60% protects LBM · 60–79% aggressive cut · ≥80% risk zone

The 290 ± 25 kJ / kg fat / day figure (Alpert, 2005) defines the theoretical upper bound on adipose lipolysis. The rate-limiting step is hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL), the primary enzyme for intracellular triglyceride hydrolysis in adipocytes.

This ceiling only applies in a fully fasted state. Any caloric intake elevates insulin, which suppresses HSL via the cAMP–PKA signalling axis: insulin activates phosphodiesterase 3B, lowering cAMP and leaving HSL dephosphorylated and inactive. In a normal eating pattern, postprandial insulin suppression restricts effective fat mobilisation for the majority of waking hours — pushing the realistic daily ceiling well below 69 kcal / kg fat.

Targeting 100% therefore creates a structural deficit that fat stores cannot fully cover. The shortfall is compensated via gluconeogenesis — catabolism of skeletal muscle protein — which elevates LBM loss disproportionately. Settings below 60% keep the deficit within what adipose tissue can realistically sustain under normal dietary conditions.

Your target body-fat percentage. The projection engine steps weight and LBM forward day by day under the Alpert-based deficit until this threshold is crossed — the resulting date and final body weight appear as the cut endpoint on both charts.

TDEE

Total Daily Energy Expenditure

The total calories you burn on an average day including all activity. Target intake and minimum intake are derived directly from this value.

How to estimate your TDEE ────────────────────────────────────── TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor, male) BMR = 10 × kg + 6.25 × cm − 5 × age + 5 BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor, female) BMR = 10 × kg + 6.25 × cm − 5 × age − 161 Activity factors 1.20 · Sedentary (desk job, little or no exercise) 1.37 · Light (1–3 workouts / week) 1.55 · Moderate (3–5 workouts / week) 1.72 · Active (6–7 workouts / week) 1.90 · Very active (physical job + daily training) Example — 80 kg · 175 cm · 30 y · male · moderate BMR = 10×80 + 6.25×175 − 5×30 + 5 = 1 749 kcal TDEE = 1 749 × 1.55 ≈ 2 711 kcal

Height

Used to calculate BMI and FFMI (Fat-Free Mass Index). FFMI = LBM ÷ height². FFMI 25 marks the observed upper bound for naturally trained athletes (Kouri et al., 1995) — the projection model applies an asymptotic cap at this limit so projections remain biologically plausible.

Data

Actions

All data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage — no account, no server, no cloud. Export a backup before clearing; the operation is irreversible.

CSV Format

The export file is plain UTF-8 CSV with three columns. Dates follow ISO 8601. Body-fat is optional — rows without a measurement export an empty field. The final #params row encodes all current settings and is fully restored on import.

date,weight,bodyfat 2025-01-01,85.40,24.5 2025-01-03,85.10, 2025-01-05,84.80,24.2 #params,agg=55,lbmpct=15,tdee=2400;alpert=69;height=175;goalBf=15;buffer=0

date — YYYY-MM-DD (ISO 8601)
weight — kilograms
bodyfat — percent, optional per row
#params — parameter snapshot; import replaces all data and settings in one step
